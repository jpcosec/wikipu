# FAQ Document

**Explanation:** New agents and humans approaching the system consistently encounter the same questions: Why does this use a graph instead of just files? What is the difference between desk/ and wiki/? When do I need human approval? Why is autopoiesis not just a cron job? These questions are answerable from the hausordnung but require reading the full document to find the answer. A FAQ short-circuits that and is indexable by graph query.

**Reference:** `wiki/standards/00_house_rules.md`, `wiki/concepts/how_wikipu_works.md`

**What to fix:** Create `wiki/reference/faq.md` as a structured Q&A node. Each entry: a question stated as an agent or human would actually ask it, a 2–4 sentence answer, and a `see_also` pointer to the authoritative rule or node.

**Questions to include (minimum):**
- Why a knowledge graph instead of just a file tree?
- What is the difference between `wiki/` and `desk/`?
- What is the difference between `wiki/` and `raw/`?
- When does an action require human approval?
- What counts as "inside the topology"?
- Why is `00_INDEX_MOC.md` renamed to `Index.md`?
- When should I commit?
- What does autopoiesis mean here, practically?
- How do I know if a node is current truth vs. planned?
- What do I do if a rule in the hausordnung causes friction?
- What is the Socratic methodology and why does this system use it?
- How do I add a new module without breaking the topology?

## Details

**Core characteristics:**
- **Self-production**: Components continuously regenerate and maintain the network that produced them
- **Operational closure**: Sufficient internal processes exist to maintain the whole system
- **Structural coupling**: The system remains embedded in dynamic interaction with its environment
- **Organizational closure**: Networks of constraints work to sustain themselves

**Key distinction — autopoietic vs. allopoietic:**
Autopoietic systems produce *themselves*. Allopoietic systems (like a car factory) produce *something other than themselves*. The distinction is whether the system's operation reproduces the conditions for its own existence or produces an external product.

**On constraint closure:**
> "Autopoiesis in biological systems can be viewed as a network of constraints that work to maintain themselves. This concept has been called organizational closure or constraint closure and is closely related to the study of autocatalytic chemical networks where constraints are reactions required to sustain life."

**On autonomy and coupling:**
> "An autopoietic system is autonomous and operationally closed, in the sense that there are sufficient processes within it to maintain the whole. Autopoietic systems are 'structurally coupled' with their medium, embedded in a dynamic of changes that can be recalled as sensory-motor coupling. This continuous dynamic is considered as a rudimentary form of knowledge or cognition."

**McGann on texts:**
> "Texts are autopoietic mechanisms operating as self-generating feedback systems that cannot be separated from those who manipulate and use them."

McGann defines an autopoietic system (citing Maturana and Varela) as:
> "A closed topological space that 'continuously generates and specifies its own organization through its operation as a system of production of its own components, and does this in an endless turnover of components'."

**Žižek on Hegel:**
> "Hegel is — to use today's terms — the ultimate thinker of autopoiesis, of the process of the emergence of necessary features out of chaotic contingency, the thinker of contingency's gradual self-organisation, of the gradual rise of order out of chaos."

**Criticism (Haraway):**
Donna Haraway argues "nothing makes itself; nothing is really autopoietic," proposing *sympoiesis* (making-with) as a preferable framework. A knowledge system maintained by humans and agents together may be more accurately described as sympoietic — co-produced — than strictly autopoietic.
